# Financial Benchmarks for Service Agent KPIs

Use these industry-referenced benchmarks to translate Business Value KPI changes into estimated
financial impact. Always present values as a range. If a source provides a single value, calculate
a range using the value +/- 10%.

---

## Cost per Contact / Cost per Case

| Benchmark | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Industry avg. cost per contact (all channels) | $6.44–$7.88 | LiveAgent Industry Benchmark (avg. $7.16 +/- 10%) |
| Retail / e-commerce / food delivery cost per ticket | $2.70–$5.60 | MaestroQA 2024 Call Center Cost Study |
| Telecom / utilities cost per ticket | $20–$30 | CX Today 2024 Contact Center Cost Analysis |
| Travel / hospitality cost per call | $10–$25 | Callin.io 2024 Inbound Call Benchmark |
| Financial services / SaaS cost per ticket | $18–$60 | LiveChatAI 2025 Cross-Industry Analysis |

**How to apply**: Multiply the per-contact cost by the number of deflected sessions to estimate
direct savings. For example, if the agent deflects 1,000 sessions/month at $2.70–$5.60 per
contact, the estimated monthly savings range is $2,700–$5,600.

## Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) Impact

| Benchmark | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1% increase in CSAT → retention increase | Up to 5% increase in retention rates | Statisfy / Industry Study |
| 5% increase in customer retention → profit increase | 25%–95% increase in profits | Bain & Company / Harvard Business Review |
| Modest CSAT improvement → revenue uptick | Significant (company-specific) | Harvard Business Review |
| 1% shift in satisfaction → loyalty shift | Up to 61.7% shift in customer loyalty | Userpilot / Industry Research |
| Customers willing to pay more for great CX | 86% of buyers | SuperOffice |

**How to apply**: Calculate the customer lifetime value (CLV), then estimate retention improvement
from CSAT gains. A 10% CSAT improvement for a company with $4,000 CLV and 1,500 at-risk customers
could represent $600,000 in retained monthly revenue (SurveyMonkey example methodology).

## First Contact Resolution (FCR) Impact

| Benchmark | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 1% FCR improvement → CSAT improvement | ~1% CSAT improvement | SQM Group FCR Benchmark Study |
| FCR improvement → repeat contact reduction | 10%–20% reduction in total case volume | SQM Group |
| E-commerce FCR benchmark | 75% | Industry Standard (call center benchmarks) |

**How to apply**: Higher FCR directly reduces repeat contacts (each avoided repeat contact saves
one full cost-per-contact). It also improves CSAT, which compounds into the retention and revenue
benefits above.

## Resolution Time Impact

| Benchmark | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| AI tools avg. time savings | ~2 hours/day per agent using generative AI | HubSpot 2024 |
| 92% of companies report AI saves time resolving issues | Qualitative | Small Contact Center Report 2024 |
| Agent replacement cost | $10,000+ per agent | AmplifAI 2026 Customer Service Statistics |

**How to apply**: Reduction in avg. handle time frees agent capacity. If resolution time drops
from 10 min to 3 min (70% reduction), each agent can handle ~3x more complex cases per shift.
Quantify as avoided headcount: (deflected volume × old avg. handle time) / shift hours = FTEs
saved.

| Benchmark | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|

| AI/self-service ROI multiplier in first year | 2x–5x | LiveChatAI 2025 Cross-Industry Analysis |

**How to apply**: Each deflected session saves the full cost-per-contact. At scale, deflection
is the largest single driver of cost optimization. Calculate: monthly deflected sessions ×
cost-per-contact range = monthly savings range.

## Usage notes

- Always cite the source name and year when referencing a benchmark in the care plan
- If the customer's industry is known, prefer the industry-specific benchmark over the general one
- For food delivery / marketplace businesses, use the Retail / E-commerce tier ($2.70–$5.60)
- Frame all financial impact as "cost optimization" or "estimated savings" — never use "ROI" or
  "return on investment"
- These benchmarks are directional estimates; actual impact depends on the customer's specific
  volume, cost structure, and operating model
